## Design Context

### Users
Financial analysts and quantitative researchers who need to extract historical Forex OHLCV data for USD currency pairs. They are technically proficient, work with large datasets, and demand precision. Context: data pipeline setup, research workflows — efficiency matters more than hand-holding.

### Brand Personality
Efficient · Precise · High-end

Emotional goals: confidence in data quality, calm focus during long download operations, trust in the tool's reliability. No friction, no noise.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Dark mode only — non-negotiable for terminal/analyst workflows
- Premium financial terminal aesthetic: think Bloomberg, Refinitiv Eikon, or a high-end trading dashboard — not a generic dev tool
- Tight, data-dense layouts with strong typographic hierarchy
- Accent colors should feel financial: cool blues or cyan with selective gold/amber for emphasis (not warm consumer-app colors)
- Anti-references: avoid playful/consumer app aesthetics, avoid bright saturated gradients, avoid rounded "bubbly" UI
- Subtle, purposeful — every visual element earns its place

### Design Principles
1. **Data first** — typography and spacing serve legibility of numbers and labels above all else
2. **Purposeful restraint** — no decoration for its own sake; silence is a design choice
3. **High-signal, low-noise** — muted base tones, reserved accent use; color communicates state, not style
4. **Premium density** — information-dense without feeling cramped; generous micro-spacing within tight macro-layout
5. **Operational clarity** — during active downloads, progress and status must be instantly readable at a glance
