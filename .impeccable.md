## Design Context

### Users
Financial analysts and quantitative researchers who need to extract historical Forex OHLCV data for USD currency pairs. They are technically proficient, work with large datasets, and demand precision. Context: data pipeline setup, research workflows — efficiency matters more than hand-holding.

### Brand Personality
Precise · Institutional · Clean

Emotional goals: confidence in data quality, calm focus during long download operations, trust in the tool's reliability. The interface should feel like a Bloomberg terminal that ran through Apple's design system — not a consumer app.

### Aesthetic Direction
**"Bloomberg Light"** — Apple's exact color discipline fused with financial terminal typographic character.

- **Font**: JetBrains Mono as the primary typeface across *everything* — titles, labels, inputs, chips, progress. Not as a "technical" decoration but as the instrument of precision this app demands.
- **Color**: Apple HIG exact tokens — `#F5F5F7` base, `#FFFFFF` cards, `#007AFF` sole accent, `#34C759` success, `#FF3B30` error. Text: `#1D1D1F` / `#6E6E73` / `#8E8E93`. Zero decorative color.
- **Radii**: 8px on cards (sharp enough to feel institutional), 6px on controls. Not the consumer-app 14px pill.
- **Shadow**: One subtle drop shadow on cards — `0 1px 3px rgba(0,0,0,0.07), 0 4px 12px rgba(0,0,0,0.05)`.
- **Layout**: Left-aligned text. Section headers with thin `1px` rule. No nested card-in-card. Breathing room where it matters, density where data lives.
- **Scroll pickers**: iOS drum-roller aesthetic with JetBrains Mono numerals, Apple fade mask, hairline selection frame.
- **Chips**: Bloomberg field identifier style — tight, uppercase ticker codes, hairline borders, blue fill on selection.

**References:** Bloomberg Terminal (typography density, field labels), Apple System Preferences (color, space, shadow), Robinhood web (light mode financial precision).
**Anti-references:** Crypto dashboards, neon-dark themes, rounded pill buttons, glassmorphism.

### Design Principles
1. **Monospace as identity** — JetBrains Mono everywhere reinforces the financial-data nature; it's not decoration, it's character
2. **Apple color discipline** — one blue, semantic red/green only for states; the palette is as restrained as a balance sheet
3. **Institutional sharpness** — 8px radii, 1px rules, 4px progress tracks — precision over friendliness
4. **Left-aligned hierarchy** — section labels left-anchored with ruled separators; the eye reads down, not hunts around
5. **Operational clarity** — during download operations, status and progress must be machine-readable at a glance
